Yes, they absolutely can, but with a major caveat regarding how they are "recharged."

Out of the three architectures, the **High-pH Metal-Air (specifically Aluminum-Air or Zinc-Air)** is the only one capable of handling this. The Acid-Base system is too hazardous and bulky for residential use, and the Enzymatic Bio-Cell only produces microwatts.

Here is exactly what it takes to run an HP Omen 45L off-grid using a Metal-Air battery.

### The Power Budget

The HP Omen 45L is a massive power draw. If it is configured with flagship hardware (like an Intel i9/Ryzen 9 and an RTX 4090 or the newer 50-series), the desktop alone will pull between 650W and 850W under a heavy gaming or rendering load.

* **The PC + Monitor:** ~800 Watts.
* **The Tiny Home Baseline:** A modern, highly efficient tiny home running a mini-fridge, LED lights, router, and occasional water pump draws a continuous average of roughly 200 Watts.

**Total Load:** You need a battery capable of outputting a continuous **1 Kilowatt (1,000 Watts)** while you are gaming.

### The Battery Size & Runtime

The main advantage of an Aluminum-Air battery is its immense energy density. While a standard Lithium-ion home battery (like a Tesla Powerwall 2) weighs about 114 kg (251 lbs) to store 13.5 kWh of energy, an Aluminum-Air system has a practical, real-world energy density of about 400 Watt-hours per kilogram.

If you build an Aluminum-Air battery pack weighing **60 kg (about 132 lbs)**, it will hold roughly **24 kWh** of usable energy.

* **Under Maximum Load:** If you run the HP Omen 45L at maximum throttle while powering the tiny home, that 60 kg battery will give you **24 hours of continuous runtime.** * **Standard Usage:** If you game or work heavily for 4 hours a day and let the PC idle (or sleep) the rest of the time, your daily energy consumption would drop to around 6 to 8 kWh. That same 60 kg battery would run the entire tiny home and the PC for **3 to 4 days** completely off-grid.

### The Catch: How You "Recharge"

The reason you don't see these in tiny homes right now is the recharge cycle.

Unlike a lithium-ion battery that you hook up to a solar array on your roof, Aluminum-Air batteries are *primary* cells. They act more like a solid-state generator. The high pH liquid actively consumes the metal to release energy.

When the 24 kWh is depleted, you cannot plug the battery into a solar panel to charge it back up. The aluminum anode has been oxidized into aluminum hydroxide. To "recharge" the battery, you have to physically open the cell, remove the spent metal sludge, and slot in fresh, solid aluminum plates.

For a permanent off-grid tiny home, keeping a stockpile of aluminum plates is logistically tougher than just relying on standard solar panels and lithium iron phosphate (LiFePO4) batteries. However, if you need a failsafe, multi-day backup system that sits silently in a closet for years without degrading until an emergency strikes, an Aluminum-Air pack is virtually unbeatable.
