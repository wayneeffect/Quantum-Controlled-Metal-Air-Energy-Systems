### 1. The Physics of the Quantum Solar Array

Classical silicon solar panels are hard-capped by the Shockley-Queisser limit, meaning they can fundamentally only convert about 33% of sunlight into usable electrical energy. The rest is lost as heat because one photon only ever excites one electron. A quantum solar array bypasses this entirely.

* **Nonlinear Multi-Exciton Generation (MEG):** In a nonlinear quantum dot lattice, a single high-energy blue or UV photon doesn't just knock one electron loose. By engineering the panel's internal structure to support quantum coherence (similar to how photosynthesis works in plants), the electron exists in a nonlocal superposition. The result is a solar array operating at potentially 60% to 80% efficiency, dumping a massive, highly stable DC voltage down the line.
